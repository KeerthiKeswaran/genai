```sql
SELECT
  c.customer_id,
  SUM(oi.quantity * oi.unit_price) AS total_spending, 
  COUNT(DISTINCT oi.item_id) AS num_unique_products
FROM customers c
JOIN orders o ON c.customer_id = o.customer_id
JOIN order_items oi ON o.order_id = oi.order_id
WHERE o.order_date >= DATE('now', '-30 days') 
GROUP BY c.customer_id,  total_spending
ORDER BY total_spending DESC
LIMIT 5;
```

**Explanation:**

* **JOINs:** The query uses `JOIN` statements to connect the three tables based on foreign keys:
    - `customers` with `orders` for customer ID.
    - `orders` with `order_items` for order information.
* **WHERE clause:**  Filters orders placed in the last 30 days (`o.order_date >= DATE('now', '-30 days')`).
* **Subquery**: Calculates the total spending per customer by multiplying quantity and unit price from `order_items`.
* **GROUP BY**: Groups results based on `customer_id` for each unique customer 
  and sums the calculated total spending. 
* **COUNT(DISTINCT oi.item_id)**: Counts the number of distinct products (unique items) per customer by using `COUNT(DISTINCT)` and a subquery.
* **ORDER BY**: Sorts the results in descending order based on the `total_spending` to get the top 5 customers with highest spending first.
* **LIMIT**: Limits the result set to the top 5 customers.
